## Causal Discovery & Structure Learning

### Causal invariance in graphical models with latent variables
**Marco Borriero, Monia Lupparelli, Giovanni M. Marchetti, Veronica Vinciotti**
[arXiv:2606.13281](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13281v1)

Extends the causal invariance principle to settings with latent variables, where the induced observed-variable graph is no longer a DAG but a maximal ancestral graph (MAG). Derives identification conditions for causal parents from invariances across environments, formalising when equivalence-class information from MAGs suffices.

*Why relevant:* Directly combines causal invariance, latent confounding, and ancestral graph theory -- core topics for structure learning in causally insufficient systems.

## Conditional Independence Testing

### Fast Nonparametric Conditional Independence Testing via Two-Stage Regression
**Eric V. Strobl**
[arXiv:2606.18011](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18011v1) | `stat.ML`, `cs.LG`, `stat.ME`

Introduces BLITZ, a fast nonparametric conditional independence test based on two-stage regression with cross-fitting. Under a generalised additive model the semiparametrically efficient test statistic reduces to a simple residual correlation, maintaining calibration with flexible ML nuisance estimators while running in under a second.

*Why relevant:* Fast, calibrated nonparametric CI testing is a core primitive for constraint-based causal discovery; semiparametric efficiency result is noteworthy for high-dimensional applications.
