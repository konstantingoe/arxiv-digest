## Causal Discovery & Structure Learning

### GRACE: Gated Refinement for Accurate Causal Edge Discovery in High-Dimensional Time Series
**Mohammad Fesanghary, Abhinav Havaldar**
[arXiv:2606.23880](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23880v1) | `cs.LG`, `stat.ME`

Proposes GRACE, which refines constraint-based causal discovery using Hard Concrete gates with L0 regularisation. A fast linear CI skeleton provides high-recall candidates; a gated model then prunes false positives by learning which edges genuinely improve prediction. On synthetic benchmarks up to d=100 and a real river-flow dataset, GRACE matches expensive nonlinear CI tests at 75x lower cost.

*Why relevant:* Directly addresses scalable CI testing and structure learning in high-dimensional time series, combining constraint-based rigour with score-based efficiency.

### Estimation of High Dimensional Bounded Discrete Graphical Models via Regularized Generalized Score Matching
**Xuran Meng, Jingfei Zhang, Yi Li**
[arXiv:2606.27142](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27142v1) | `stat.ME`

Introduces bounded discrete graphical models for finite-support count data and a regularised generalised score matching estimator (BRIDGE) that avoids normalising-constant computation. Analyses a nonconvex objective via a population separation property, yielding nonasymptotic error bounds and exact support recovery guarantees in high dimensions.

*Why relevant:* High-dimensional graphical model estimation with provable structure recovery; relevant to conditional dependence learning and neighbourhood selection.

### Causal Discovery in the Era of Agents
**Yujia Zheng, Vishal Verma, Mantej Gill, Haoyue Dai, Peter Spirtes, Kun Zhang**
[arXiv:2606.23608](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23608v1) | `cs.AI`, `cs.LG`, `cs.SE`, `stat.AP`

Argues that LLM agents should assist causal discovery workflows (data inspection, method recommendation, interpretation) but should not supply edges, orientations, or causal conclusions. Instantiates this principle in causal-learn+, an online platform coordinating preprocessing, expert-knowledge incorporation, and formal discovery around the causal-learn ecosystem.

*Why relevant:* Proposes a principled framework for integrating LLM agents with formal causal discovery algorithms; authored by Spirtes and Zhang.

### One Ruler: A Same-Hands Re-Evaluation of Bivariate Causal Direction on Tuebingen
**Wietse Stienstra**
[arXiv:2606.23767](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23767v1) | `cs.LG`

Runs a uniform re-evaluation of bivariate causal direction methods on all 102 Tuebingen pairs under a single strict protocol (no tuning, decision forced on every pair). Introduces a zero-parameter compression baseline that ties the strongest existing methods in the low-to-mid 70s, and documents mechanisms (test-set model selection, significance-gated abstention) that inflate published figures.

*Why relevant:* Rigorous benchmarking of bivariate causal discovery methods; identifies systematic evaluation biases in the literature.

## Graphical Models, Identifiability & Causal Representation

### Semiparametric Inference for Half-Trek Estimators in Linear Structural Equation Models
**Leopold Mareis, Nils Sturma, Mathias Drton**
[arXiv:2606.26931](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26931v1) | `stat.ME`, `math.ST`

Derives the semiparametric influence function of the half-trek criterion (HTC) estimator for all HTC-identified directed mixed graphs, including cyclic ones. The HTC estimator is shown to be asymptotically normal with closed-form variance, yielding confidence regions and Wald tests for individual structural coefficients.

*Why relevant:* Provides rigorous inference theory for identifiable linear SEMs on directed mixed graphs under latent confounding -- core methodology for graphical causal models.

### Disentangling Continuous-Time Latent Dynamics: Identifiability of Latent SDEs via Diffusion Shifts
**Yuanyuan Wang, Wenjie Wang, Haoxuan Li, Mingming Gong, Kun Zhang**
[arXiv:2606.28228](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28228v1) | `cs.LG`, `stat.ML`

Establishes identifiability for additive-noise latent SDEs observed through an unknown nonlinear diffeomorphism, using environment-induced shifts in diffusion covariance. Two diagonal diffusion regimes with distinct variance ratios identify latent coordinates up to permutation/scaling without sparsity assumptions; the drift-Jacobian causal graph is then identifiable.

*Why relevant:* Extends causal representation identifiability to continuous-time latent models; connects environment-based identification with causal graph recovery.

### Infinitesimal Causality
**Sridhar Mahadevan**
[arXiv:2606.24621](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24621v1) | `math.CT`, `cs.AI`, `math.ST`

Develops a categorical account of infinitesimal causality in Frobenius Markov categories with tangent-bundle semantics. Interventions are formalised as tangent deformations of copy/discard structure; Pearl's do-calculus rules (ignoring irrelevant interventions, action/observation exchange, independence) are recovered as counit invariance, coproduct compatibility, and involutive bracket closure.

*Why relevant:* Novel categorical-geometric foundations for interventions and do-calculus; provides a unified algebraic framework for causal sufficiency.

## Interventions & Experimental Design

### Optimizing Experimental Design for Causal Effect Estimation with Partial Measurements
**Leopold Mareis**
[arXiv:2606.26818](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26818v1) | `stat.ME`, `math.ST`

Shows that in Gaussian graphical models with an instrumental variable, collecting partial samples (e.g. from only two of three variables) can reduce the asymptotic variance of a consistent IV estimator compared to always measuring all variables. Provides an analytically solvable budget-constrained optimisation for the optimal mix of partial and complete samples, with significance level, power, and sample-size calculations.

*Why relevant:* Optimal experimental design for causal effect estimation in graphical models with instruments; directly relevant to interventional design and cost-efficient causal inference.

**Wenxuan Guo, Tengyuan Liang**
[arXiv:2606.28200](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28200v1) | `stat.ME`

**Hengshi Yu**
[arXiv:2606.26630](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26630v1) | `stat.ME`

Proposes DR-QIF, combining doubly robust pseudo-outcomes with quadratic inference function score equations for average treatment effect estimation in cluster randomised trials. *Why relevant:* Advances doubly robust estimation methodology for clustered data; relevant to semiparametric causal inference with correlated observations.
