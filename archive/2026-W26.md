## Causal Discovery & Structure Learning

### Latent Confounded Causal Discovery via Lie Bracket Geometry
**Sridhar Mahadevan**
[arXiv:2606.19610](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19610v1) | `cs.LG`, `cs.AI`

Introduces two causal discovery algorithms for settings with latent confounding based on differential geometry. Observational-vs-interventional density ratios induce causal vector fields whose Lie-bracket closures detect latent structure; the BRIDGE algorithm uses Frobenius residuals to screen for hidden confounders, while Spectral Kan-Do Flow Matching factors latent curvature spectrally.

*Why relevant:* Novel geometric approach to causal discovery under latent confounding; directly addresses DAG search-space reduction and latent variable detection.

**Praharsh Nanavati, Jilles Vreeken, David Kaltenpoth**
[arXiv:2606.18834](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18834v1) | `cs.LG`

**Yordan P. Raykov, Hengrui Luo, Justin D. Strait, Wasiur R. KhudaBukhsh**
[arXiv:2606.18535](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18535v1) | `stat.ME`, `cs.LG`, `math.ST`

Develops a Bayesian factor model with shrinkage priors for learning sparse substitute confounders in multi-cause observational studies. [arXiv:2606.18459](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18459v1) | `stat.ME`

Proposes a framework for retrospectively apportioning causal responsibility between two binary risk factors using potential outcomes.
